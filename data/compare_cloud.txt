# US Federal Rules for Prescribing Controlled Substances via Telehealth

---

**1. Baseline Federal Law: The Ryan Haight Act**

The Ryan Haight Online Pharmacy Consumer Protection Act is the foundational federal law governing online prescribing of controlled substances. It requires **at least one in-person evaluation** before a provider may prescribe controlled substances (Schedules II–V) online. [Record ID: 373]

This law remains in effect, but COVID-era telehealth flexibilities have **temporarily waived** this in-person requirement. [Record ID: 373]

---

**2. Current Temporary Waiver: DEA/HHS 4th Extension (Through Dec 31, 2026)**

The DEA and HHS issued a **4th Temporary Rule** extending telehealth flexibilities for prescribing Schedule II–V controlled substances **through December 31, 2026**. Key provisions include:

- **All DEA-registered providers** may prescribe controlled substances via telehealth. [Record ID: 371]
- This applies **regardless of when the provider-patient relationship was formed** — meaning no prior in-person visit is required during the extension period. [Record ID: 371]
- The extension is intended to provide time for the DEA to **finalize a permanent Special Registration for Telemedicine**, which would create a long-term legal pathway around the Ryan Haight Act's in-person requirement for qualified providers. [Record ID: 371] [Record ID: 373]

---

**3. Interaction with Medicare Telehealth Flexibilities**

Congress extended broader Medicare telehealth flexibilities through **December 31, 2027** via the Consolidated Appropriations Act 2026 (H.R. 7148, signed Feb 3, 2026). This includes waiving in-person requirements for **behavioral telehealth**, allowing the **home as an originating site**, geographic waivers, and expanded clinician eligibility. [Record ID: 370]

*Inference (not directly stated in any record):* The Medicare telehealth extension (through 2027) and the DEA controlled substances extension (through 2026) are on **different timelines**, which could create a gap in 2027 where Medicare covers telehealth visits broadly but DEA rules may revert to requiring in-person evaluations for controlled substances — unless the permanent Special Registration is finalized or another extension is issued.

---

**4. Proposed Permanent Fix: CONNECT for Health Act**

The **CONNECT for Health Act** has been proposed in Congress to make Medicare telehealth flexibilities permanent, removing geographic and originating site restrictions and eliminating the need for recurring temporary extensions. However, this bill has **not yet been enacted** as of early 2026. [Record ID: 372]

---

**5. State-Level Variation**

Beyond federal rules, states impose their own additional requirements on telehealth prescribing. Some states require an initial in-person visit before prescribing, others allow prescribing after a video-only encounter, and some permit audio-only prescribing for non-controlled substances. For controlled substances specifically, state rules operate **on top of** the federal Ryan Haight Act and DEA temporary flexibilities. [Record ID: 389]

---

**6. HIPAA Compliance**

All telehealth encounters — including those involving controlled substance prescribing — must use **HIPAA-compliant communication platforms**. The earlier COVID-era OCR enforcement discretion for non-compliant platforms has **expired**. [Record ID: 375]
